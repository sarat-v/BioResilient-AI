Scientific Methodology

Completed Evidence Layers

  • Whole-proteome ortholog clustering via OrthoFinder (DIAMOND + MCL) — all 19 species
  • MAFFT multiple sequence alignment for ~11k conserved orthogroups
  • Sliding-window divergent motif detection in cancer-resistant vs. control branches
  • Pfam/InterPro domain annotation of all divergent regions
  • gnomAD pLI/LOEUF constraint scoring on human gene orthologs
  • ESM-1v deep learning pathogenicity scoring for cancer-lineage variants
  • IQ-TREE2 maximum-likelihood species tree (GTR+G, 1000 bootstraps)
  • Nucleotide conservation (Jukes-Cantor) and phylogenetic conservation scores

Running Now — Step 6 HyPhy Suite

  • BUSTED-PH — BUSTED restricted to cancer-resistant foreground branches. Gene-level LRT: "Is selection specifically elevated in resistant lineages?" ~100× faster than MEME, same phenotype specificity
  • FEL (Fixed Effects Likelihood) — Detects pervasive positive selection at individual codon sites across all lineages
  • BUSTED — Gene-wide episodic selection test; identifies whether any lineage drove positive selection
  • RELAX — Tests whether selection intensity is intensified (k>1) or relaxed (k<1) in cancer-resistant vs. reference branches

Scoring & Ranking (Post Step 6)

  • Composite selection score: FEL 35% · BUSTED 30% · BUSTED-PH 25% · RELAX 10%
  • Convergence score: weighted by phylogenetic distance between independently-evolving resistant lineages (more phylogenetically distant convergences score higher)
  • Rank-product integration (Breitling et al. 2004) across all evidence layers with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction
  • Gene tiers: Tier 1 (FDR <5%), Tier 2 (FDR <20%), Tier 3 (nominal candidates)
Scientific Rationale
🎯

The core hypothesis: Cancer resistance evolved independently in multiple distantly-related lineages — naked mole-rats, bowhead whales, elephants, sharks, turtles, and others. Genes that show positive selection specifically in these lineages, across 7 independent evolutionary events, are strong candidates for novel cancer-suppressive mechanisms.

Why this matters: Unlike single-species studies (e.g. the elephant TP53 story), we are looking for convergent molecular evolution — the same protein regions adapting in the same direction in unrelated species. These convergent signals are much less likely to be noise and point to mechanisms under universal selective pressure.

What Step 6 adds: The protein-level motif analysis (Steps 4) finds what changed in sequence. Step 6 adds why it changed — distinguishing functional adaptation (positive selection) from neutral drift. A gene with both a convergent amino-acid substitution AND elevated selection pressure in cancer-resistant lineages is the gold standard hit.
